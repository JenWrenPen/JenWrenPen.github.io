In May 2025, I successfully defended my doctoral work at NTNU, Trondheim. I fell in love with the city and now call it home. My doctoral work focussed on decay behaviour, escape rates and state transitions in simple models for complex systems. I am skilled in data analysis, creating simulations and computational modelling. My creativity allows me to seek (and quickly find) simple solutions to problems and present my work in clear engaging ways. In my most recent employment, I put these skills to work as a temporary researcher with a responsibility for creating outreach materials.

NTNU PhD in Engineering
The label of "PhD in Engineering" comes from the department I worked in. A better umbrella term for my work is applied mathematics. My thesis is titled "Decay behaviour, escape rates and state transitions in simple models for complex systems" and can be found here. This involved applying mathematical principles and modelling to two different systems: nanotribological phenomena of atomically thin layered materials and drug resistance in chronic myeloid leukaemia treatment. As par for the course of completing my PhD, I published three papers (more information can be found on my science page) and attended conferences. After presenting at conferences, I frequently received praise for the high level of pedagogy and quality of animations in my presentations.
