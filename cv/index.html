PhD in Engineering


The label of "PhD in Engineering" comes from the faculty I worked in. A better umbrella-term for my work is applied mathematics. My thesis is titled "Decay behaviour, escape rates and state transitions in simple models for complex systems" and can be found here. This involved applying mathematical principles and modelling methods to two different systems: nanotribological phenomena of atomically thin layered materials and drug resistance in chronic myeloid leukaemia treatment. As par for the course of completing my PhD, I attended conferences and published three papers (details of which can be found on my science page). After presenting at conferences, I frequently received praise for the high level of pedagogy and quality of animations in my presentations.

University of Liverpool

MSc in Radiometrics: Instrumentation and Modelling – Distinction


This course covered the theoretical basics of ionising radiation (its production and detection); the set-up, calibration and use of detector systems; and the creation of models of detector set-ups, which were then validated through practical experiment.
I took a particular interest in the human factors of ionising radiation and chose this as the topic of my dissertation. This focussed on the importance and creation of risk assessments. For my project I created a successful model of a novel mobile compton camera using Geant4 and collected data to validate the model.
Along with Geant4, I also became skilled in modelling using MATLAB and MCNP, and collected data using ProSpect and Maestro software.
